Our Mission

data

Texas Instruments has had a strong grip on the calculator market for pretty much as long as we remember. A TI-84, a standard graphing calculator costs nearly $116, but Texas instruments main competitors Casio and HP have calculators with the same functions which cost significantly less. Seeing as 60% of americans could not withstand a $1000 emergency, a burden of $115 dollars for a calculator certainly isn’t palatable. The problem is that many students and teachers don’t know how to use these calculators, decreasing competition and adding financial burden. We have built a website that can show kids how to use cheaper competitor calculators to reduce the financial strain on low income families and to help increase innovation in the calculator market.
